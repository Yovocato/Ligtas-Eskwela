WHAT ARE FIRES?

Fire is a chemical reaction that happens when something burns. It needs three things to start: heat, fuel (something that can burn), and oxygen from the air. When these come together, fire produces light, heat, and flames. Fire gives off light and warmth, but it can also be dangerous if not handled carefully.

WHAT TO DO DURING FIRES?

BEFORE

  • The key to staying safe is to prepare and to have an emergency plan.
  • Make sure everyone knows where the fire extinguishers are kept.
  • Keep flammable items at least one metre from heat sources.
  • Don't overload electrical outlets.

DURING

  • Activate the fire alarm to alert people inside the building.
  • Do treat the fire alarm as an emergency and exit the building immediately.
  • If closed doors or handles are warm, use an alternate exit.
  • Crawl under low smoke.
  • Close the doors before leaving the premises as this would help in slowing the fire and decreasing the damage.

AFTER

  • Ensure your food and water is safe. Discard any food that may have been exposed to heat, smoke or soot and do not use water that may be contaminated.
  • Once in a safe place, call or report about the fire outbreak and inform about your safety/assistance required.
  • Await further instructions from the fire emergency department and stay safe in the refuge area.

During a Fire

Alert: Immediately alert others and activate fire alarms if available.
Escape: Follow your evacuation plan and exit the building quickly.
Call for Help: Once safe, call emergency services immediately.
