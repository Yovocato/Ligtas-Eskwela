WHAT IS AN EARTHQUAKE?

An earthquake is basically the ground shaking in different numbers of magnitudes. There are plenty of instances to trigger an earthquake such as an eruption, tectonic plates moving, etc. Earthquakes are unpredictable and dangerous enough to be a hazard depending on the magnitude so it's important to stay informed and prepared.

WHAT TO DO DURING EARTHQUAKE?

BEFORE

  • Prepare an emergency kit with food, water, flashlight, and first aid supplies.
  • Heavy items and furniture should be secured to avoid falling.
  • Find secure areas in every space, such as against interior walls or behind heavy tables.
  • Plan an evacuation route and practice earthquake drills with your family.

DURING

  • Duck, Cover and Hold: Find a substantial piece of furniture to shield your head.
  • If you're indoors, stay away from mirrors, windows, and heavy items.
  • If you're outside, find a space that is open and free of trees, buildings, and power lines.
  • If you're driving, stop in a safe spot and stay away from overpasses, bridges, and tunnels.
  • Keep your composure and reassure everyone around you, especially the old and young people.

AFTER

  • Check yourself and others for injuries.
  • Be prepared for aftershocks.
  • Stay away from damaged buildings and areas.
  • Help those in need if it's safe to do so.
  • Follow instructions from authorities and emergency personnel.

During an Earthquake

Drop: Get down on your hands and knees to protect yourself from being knocked over.
Cover: Take cover under a sturdy table or piece of furniture. Protect your head and neck.
Hold On: Hold on to your shelter until the shaking stops. Stay away from windows and glass.

WHAT TO BRING?

First-Aid Kit

Flashlight

Foods and Water

Clothes and sturdy shoes

Whistle for emergencies
