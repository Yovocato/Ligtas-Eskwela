WHAT IS A FLOOD?

Think of it like this, there's a big storm, and it's raining too hard. The sewers in your area cannot hold all of the built up water, so the water spills out into the roads, buildings, and eventually your homes, that is what a flood is. Floodwater is very dirty, carries mud, rocks, trash, and even harmful items, therefore you should NOT even attempt to touch a flood, and avoid situations that involve you having to walk through one.

WHAT TO DO DURING FLOOD?

BEFORE

  • Watch the news, especially the weather forecast, you should advise your other family members to do the same!
  • Keep your gadgets phones, tablets, other electronics and your belongings on high places so they don't get wet!
  • Try to look around your house, know where the proper exits are.
  • Pack a bag with important things (food, water, flashlight, etc.)

DURING

  • Stay inside, avoid touching the flooding water.
  • If possible, stay in higher places especially if you have a tall house!
  • Avoid touching anything electric, it is likely wet which is dangerous.
  • Listen to the adults, parents, emergency responders, etc.

AFTER

  • DON'T go walking anywhere outside yet! Wait for announcements relating to an "all clear" by staff, because that means it's safe once they declare it.
  • If there is any leftover floodwater, just don't touch it.
  • Be careful or try to avoid any damaged buildings/houses for the meantime.
  • You can help clean up, but do it ONLY when your safety is ensured.

During a Flood

Move to Higher Ground: Immediately move to higher elevations if flooding is imminent.
Avoid Floodwater: Never walk or drive through flood waters.
Stay Informed: Listen to official instructions and emergency alerts.

WHAT TO BRING?

Foods and Water

Clothes

First-aid kit

Hygiene kit

Blanket

Flashlight
