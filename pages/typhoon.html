WHAT IS A TYPHOON?

These are strong winds, heavy rain, and dark skies that signal the arrival of a powerful storm. A typhoon is a massive rotating storm that forms over warm ocean waters and brings intense winds, heavy rainfall, and flooding. It can cause destruction by uprooting trees, damaging houses, and leading to dangerous floods. Because of its strength, a typhoon can be very dangerous, so it's important to stay informed and prepared.

WHAT TO DO DURING TYPHOON?

BEFORE

  • Watch the news, especially weather updates, and inform your family.
  • Move important belongings and electronics to higher places to keep them dry.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with food, water, flashlight, and first aid supplies.
  • Plan an evacuation route and know the nearest safe shelter.

DURING

  • Stay indoors and away from windows to avoid flying debris.
  • If possible, move to higher ground if you are in a flood-prone area.
  • Avoid using electrical appliances, as power surges or water exposure may cause electrocution.
  • Listen to updates from authorities and follow evacuation orders if necessary.
  • Keep calm and reassure your family members, especially younger ones.

AFTER

  • Stay inside until authorities say it is safe to go out.
  • Watch out for fallen power lines, debris, and weakened structures.
  • Avoid floodwaters, as they may be contaminated or hide dangers like sharp objects.
  • Help in cleaning up, but only if it's safe to do so.
  • Check on family, friends, and neighbors to ensure everyone is safe.

During a Typhoon

Stay Inside: Remain indoors and away from windows and glass doors.
Be Prepared: Keep your emergency kit ready with food, water, and essential supplies.
Stay Informed: Monitor weather updates and follow evacuation orders if given.
